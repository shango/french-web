Questions

The honest answers

Am I a beginner or a restarter, does it matter?
Both are welcome, and the app adapts. If you're starting from zero, you get the cognate windfall and a gentle first session. If you remember some school French, take the two-minute placement (“prove it, skip it”) and you'll start further along instead of re-learning bonjour.
Do I need grammar first?
No, that's the point. A working base of words comes first; it makes grammar far easier to learn later, because you'll have real sentences to hang the rules on. When you graduate we point you to a proper A1 grammar course as your next step.
How long until I have a base?
About 4–6 months of steady, short sessions to reach ~400 words. We won't pretend it's a weekend. But the early wins come fast: the cognate windfall on day one, your first sealed deck within a couple of weeks, so it never feels five months away.
What happens when I finish?
You graduate. You get a Level-1 certificate, an export of every word you learned (CSV or Anki), and honest recommendations for what's next: a grammar course, graded readers, or a tutor. Then you can stop paying with our blessing. Leaving is the win.
Why not just use Duolingo?
Use it if you love it. The difference is intent: streak-and-points apps are designed to keep you playing indefinitely. Le Cahier is designed to end: a focused base of high-frequency words, taught with gender and in context, then a clean handoff. No hearts, no leaderboards, no guilt.
Does it work offline, on iOS and Android?
Yes, iOS, Android, and the web, with your progress in sync. Much of a session works offline for the metro or a plane; audio caches for the decks you're studying.
